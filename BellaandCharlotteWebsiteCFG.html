About Us

What We Do

We are a non-profit organisation that provides you with targeted and up-to-date advice on how to keep your data safe whilst using your Smart Home Devices. We do this by analysing the safety and security of various popular home device products and how to protect your data/information whilst using them. We also have a News Page that keeps you up-to-date with security breaches in home device products, whilst also providing a 'Product of the Week,' showcasing a home device that we consider 'safe to use.' In an age where technology and software development has become so complex, we often find that we do not fully understand the capability and thus safety of the technology devices that we use every day. Consequently, our security and awareness of these devices have never been so important when it comes to protecting our data and personal information. We believe that cyber-security must be malleable to the rapid and continuous advancement of technology and the development of technological products. As such, we hope that by making users aware of how to use home devices safely, we are able to maximise the protection of privacy and data.

Device Advice

There has been a shift between the use of traditional household items, and the rise of connected devices. Services will cease to function if devices are switched off, and we are not quite sure just how much of our personal information is gathered.. A trade off that many will most likely risk. Following these simple tips will help you to keep your device security and data privacy safe.

Always read the instructions and take time to read the terms and conditions before accepting them.

During set up, change default passwords and create a strong and memorable password.

Disable voice purchasing, this will prevent hackers from purchasing items from your account.

If possible set up a Two Factor Authentication as an additional layer of protection. Check for software and app updates. If there is an option for automatic updates enable them, this saves you the worry!

Visit the manufacturers website to check for any guidence.

Be sure to check on privacy and government websites offering guidence for consumers.

Speakers will listen to their 'wake word', and doorbells watch you. If want complete privacy or are worried about a vulnerability, Simply turn it off!
